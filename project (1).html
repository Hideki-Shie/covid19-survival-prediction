Comparing the Two Models¶

The behaviors of the models were the same. Both models had the same amount of true positives, false positives, false negatives, and true negatives. As a result of this, they also had the same weighted average of precision, recall, and f-1 score.

The weighted average of precision we got was 0.97, which means that out of all the predicted positives, 97% were actually correct. The weighted average of recall we got was 0.98, which means that out of all of the actual positives, the models correctly predicted 98% of them. The weighted average of the F-1 score we got from the models was 0.97, meaning both models had a good balance between precision and recall. This that there were few false positives and false negatives.

Both models performing the same way means that the default parameters of C = 1.0, penalty = l2, and solver = liblinear were the optimal parameters for this project.
